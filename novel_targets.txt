1. Heimdallarchaeial Rhodopsin (HeimdallR1)
HeimdallR1 is a proton-pumping rhodopsin identified in 'Candidatus Heimdallarchaeia', an Asgard archaeon of significant evolutionary interest. It is notable for its predicted "fenestration," a structural opening that allows it to bind with carotenoid antennas like fucoxanthin and lutein, enhancing its light-harvesting capabilities.  

While the full FASTA sequence is not explicitly detailed in the reviewed literature, its key identifiers have been deposited in public databases:

Organism: Candidatus Heimdallarchaeota archaeon   
UniProt Accession: A0A2E9CEV6   
PDB ID: 9JTQ   
These identifiers can be used to retrieve the full sequence from their respective databases.

2. Apusomonad Rhodopsins (ApuRs)
ApuRs are a recently discovered and entirely new family of anion-selective rhodopsin channels found exclusively in apusomonads, a group of protists. Their discovery is significant as they represent a case of convergent evolution, having developed channel functionality independently from other known channelrhodopsins.  

The literature describes a family of twelve non-redundant ApuR proteins, which exhibit high sequence diversity. Specific examples that have been identified include:  

TtApuR from Thecamonas trahens
SfApuR1 and SfApuR2 from Singekia franciliensis
PkApuR from Podomonas kaiyoae
TaraApuR from the Tara Oceans metagenome   
The full protein sequences for this family have been deposited in a public repository (figshare) by the authors of the discovery paper, but they are not listed within the body of the reviewed articles.  

3. CryoRhodopsins (CryoR1 and CryoR2)
CryoRhodopsins are a novel clade of rhodopsins identified in cold-adapted bacteria from environments such as glaciers. They are distinguished by a unique buried arginine residue and exhibit an unprecedented dual functionality, capable of switching between an inward proton pump and a photosensor, a process modulated by UV light.  

The full protein sequences for the two characterized examples, CryoR1 and CryoR2, are available through their database accession numbers.

CryoR2:
